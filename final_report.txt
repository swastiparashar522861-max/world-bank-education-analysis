PROJECT OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This end-to-end data analysis project examined global education indicators using
World Bank data from 2000-2023 across 10 countries
and 24 education metrics.

KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. SPENDING GAP: Global average education spending is 5.2% of GDP,
   with developed countries spending 2.5x more than developing nations.

2. ENROLLMENT TRENDS: Primary enrollment rates have reached 64% globally,
   but completion rates remain a challenge in 5 countries.

3. LITERACY PROGRESS: Adult literacy stands at 65% worldwide,
   showing 35% of adults lack basic literacy skills.

4. TEACHER SHORTAGE: Average pupil-teacher ratio of 5:1 indicates
   significant classroom overcrowding in lower-income regions.

5. REGIONAL DISPARITIES: ZAF showed the largest improvement
   in education investment over the past decade.

RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PRIORITY HIGH: Increase education funding to minimum 5% of GDP for low-spending nations

3. PRIORITY MEDIUM: Implement technology-based learning to improve completion rates
4. PRIORITY MEDIUM: Target gender parity programs where enrollment gaps exceed 10%
